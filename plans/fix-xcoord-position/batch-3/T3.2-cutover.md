# T3.2 — Cut over: rules corpus becomes the survey

## Context
After the headless rules corpus (T0.2) is green and the API/production wiring
(T1.1/T3.1) is in place, retire the side-by-side duplication (ADR-3): the
reference-vs-headless rules survey becomes THE layout survey. The pango-goldens
survey (LUT vs pango) is retired because its kerning gap is now covered by the
Batch-2 measurement tests, not the layout corpus.

## Task
1. Verify the rules survey is byte-exact across the FULL corpus (modulo the
   documented `rules-known-divergences.md` allowlist).
2. Make the rules survey the default CI survey:
   - point `npm run survey`/CI at the headless rules corpus + `EstimateTextMeasurer`;
   - remove or archive the old pango-goldens survey + its goldens (git history
     retains them). Update any docs/scripts that referenced the old survey.
3. Confirm the Batch-2 measurement tests cover the fidelity that the old survey
   incidentally exercised (kerning/charset) so nothing is lost.
4. Final full-gate run; update `decision-journal.md` with the cutover tally.

## Read-set
- `test/corpus/*` (old survey + new rules survey), CI config / `package.json`
  scripts, DESIGN.md §5, ADR-3

## Acceptance criteria (Given/When/Then)
- Given the full corpus, when the rules survey runs (reference vs headless), then
  byte-exact except the documented allowlist (font-independent, pre-existing).
- Given CI, when run, then the rules survey is the layout gate and is
  deterministic across platforms (no font-stack dependence).
- Given the old pango survey, then it is retired and its incidental coverage
  (kerning/charset) is replaced by Batch-2 measurement tests.
- `npm test`, `npm run typecheck`, lizard → pass/clean.

## Stop condition
If the full-corpus rules survey is NOT byte-exact after isolating pre-existing
font-independent divergences, STOP — do not cut over; the decoupling is incomplete.

## Quality bar
Merge commit (mission branch). Commit `test(corpus): cut over to deterministic
rules survey; retire pango-goldens survey`.
